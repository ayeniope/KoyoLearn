5 Tips For Living With A Roomate In School

Gaining admission to a tertiary institution is a great opportunity to gain new knowledge, experience, skills, and friends. You might not be able to afford to live alone, which means you have to live in the hostel or share a room with someone off-campus to split the rent. Your roommate could be your friend or a total stranger but they are all people you've not lived with before and you don't have any idea about their habits or behaviours at home. Don't be scared, living with a roommate could be a pleasant experience if you handle it with maturity.

Here are 5 tips to help you enjoy living with a roommate:

  • Don't Expect Too Much

    Do not have the expectation that your roommate will be your best friend and he will be perfect. You will be disappointed and those feelings could ruin your relationship from the get-go. Not everyone finds a best friend in their roommates and nobody is perfect, not even you. What you should do is plan to have a good relationship with him or her regardless of his character.

  • Establish Good Communication

    Be the first to reach out. Ask for his phone number. Get to know more about him and his family. Being in a place where you can't greet or talk to the person you live with is horrible so make sure the communication between you is great. When misunderstandings occur, the foundation of good communication will help you to resolve them quickly.

  • Discuss Your Differences

    Another man's meat is another man's poison. The person you're going to live with comes from a different family and background so your lifestyles won't be the same. You need to sit down and discuss the things that make you different from each other. Like your eating habits, you might like eating pepper and he hates it. He might like eating food with lots of onions but you're allergic to it. He might even practice a different religion. You need to be aware of all these differences to know how to live with him in peace and tolerance.

  • Set Boundaries

    Boundaries need to be set. You might be a private person who doesn't like sharing his belongings. If you need to make rules about it to prevent misunderstandings, do so. Set boundaries about visitors coming to your house. Also, you should make it known to your roommate if you prefer solitude and quiet when you're at home. He would know that he has to do things like play loud music or invite friends over when you're not around.

  • Love Your Neighbour As Yourself

    This is the last tip that sums everything up. Treat your roommate the way you would like to be treated. If you want to be respected, give him respect. If you want friendship, be friendly. Show empathy. Show love. You will get it back. And if you don't, you'll know you tried your best so you can be at peace with yourself
